⚪ What is the maximum theoretical energy efficiency of a fuel cell?

Correct Answer: 83 %


Explanation: E°cell of an H2-O2 fuel cell = 1.23 V and ΔH=-285.8 kJ mol-1
ΔG = -n x F x E°cell = -2 x 96500 x 1.23
= -237390 J mol-1
= -237.390 kJ mol-1
Efficiency = ΔG/ΔH x 100
= -237.390/-285.8 x 100
= 83%.
